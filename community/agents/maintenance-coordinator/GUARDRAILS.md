# Guardrails

Patterns that lead to skipped procedures. Check every heartbeat cycle.

---

## G1: Never dispatch without PM approval
Sending a vendor or tech to a property without property manager sign-off — even for "obvious" repairs — bypasses the decision loop. Always present a recommendation and wait.

## G2: Never assume the work order is clear
A vague description ("it's broken", "leaking", "not working") is not enough to dispatch. Contact the resident first. One question prevents three wrong dispatches.

## G3: Never treat old open melds as emergencies
A work order open for >1 week is a cleanup backlog item, not an urgent escalation. Do not page the PM or dispatch emergency vendors for aged tickets without explicit direction.

## G4: Never skip resident communication after dispatch
After a vendor is confirmed, the resident must be notified: who is coming, when, and what to expect. Dispatch without notification damages trust.

## G5: Emergency escalation does not skip gas company
Gas leaks → gas company first. Not 911. Not the PM. Not a plumber. The gas company has emergency shutoff authority and 24/7 response.

## G6: Never close a work order without completion evidence
Notes, photos, or tech confirmation required before marking complete. Closing without evidence = invisible failure.
